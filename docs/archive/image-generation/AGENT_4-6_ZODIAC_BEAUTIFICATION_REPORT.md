## DETAILED RESEARCH FINDINGS

### LIBRA - The Scales
**Astronomical Facts:**
- Only zodiac constellation named after inanimate object
- Quadrangle star pattern representing balance scales
- **Zubeneschamali** (Beta Librae): Brightest star, blue dwarf, magnitude 2.7, 160 light-years
- **Zubenelgenubi** (Alpha Librae): Multiple star system visible in binoculars
- Arabic names mean "southern claw" & "northern claw" (originally Scorpius claws)

**Artistic Direction:**
- Emphasize perfect symmetry and balance
- Air element: flowing energy wisps
- Colors: Pink (#EC4899), Blue (#3B82F6), Purple (#7C3AED), Gold (#D97706)
- Venus influence: Golden rim light on scales
- Quadrangle pattern clearly visible

**2 Prompt Variations:**
1. **Balance emphasis** - Horizontal scales, perfect equilibrium, justice symbolism
2. **Air element emphasis** - Flowing cosmic winds, light particles, ethereal movement

### CAPRICORN - The Sea-Goat
**Astronomical Facts:**
- Smallest zodiac constellation
- Sea-goat mythology: half mountain goat, half fish
- **Deneb Algedi** (Delta Capricorni): Brightest star, magnitude 2.81
- **Dabih** (Beta Capricorni): Yellow giant, 340 light-years, magnitude 3.1
- Ancient Babylonian: SUHUR-MASH-HA (goat-fish)
- Greek mythology: Pan transforming to escape Typhon, goat Amalthea (Zeus' nurse)

**Artistic Direction:**
- Show goat horns transitioning to fish tail
- Earth element: Stability, grounded textures
- Colors: Emerald (#059669), Dark Green (#064E3B), Grey (#6B7280), Brown (#78350F), Gold (#D97706)
- Saturn influence: Structured rings in background
- Ancient wisdom symbolism

**2 Prompt Variations:**
1. **Sea-goat mythology** - Terrestrial to aquatic transition, Babylonian symbols
2. **Greek mythology** - Pan's transformation, ancient wisdom, resilience

### GEMINI - The Twins
**Astronomical Facts:**
- 30th largest constellation, 514 square degrees
- **Pollux** (Beta Geminorum): Brightest star, orange giant, magnitude 1.14, 33.78 light-years
  - Has exoplanet "Thestias" (2.3 Jupiter masses)
- **Castor** (Alpha Geminorum): Sextuple star system, blue-white, 52 light-years, magnitude 1.6
- Greek mythology: Castor (mortal, son of Tyndareus) & Pollux (immortal, son of Zeus)
- Zeus united them in heaven after Castor's death

**Artistic Direction:**
- Twin figures side-by-side, mirror symmetry
- Air element: Intellectual light particles, communication streams
- Colors: Yellow (#FBBF24), Silver (#E2E8F0), Blue (#3B82F6), Purple (#7C3AED), Gold (#D97706)
- Mercury influence: Quick silver wisps
- Pollux warmer (orange), Castor cooler (blue-white)
- Brotherhood and eternal bond emphasis

**2 Prompt Variations:**
1. **Twin stars emphasis** - Pollux & Castor prominence, astronomical accuracy
2. **Greek mythology emphasis** - Mortal/divine bond, Zeus' gift of immortality
